3. Introductory Statement and General Investigational Plan

3.1 Drug Product Overview

RX-7890 is a novel, orally bioavailable small molecule inhibitor targeting the oncogenic kinase pathway. The compound demonstrates selective inhibition of tumor cell proliferation with minimal effects on normal cells.

3.2 Therapeutic Rationale

The targeted kinase pathway is overexpressed in approximately 40% of solid tumors and is associated with poor prognosis. Current therapies have limited efficacy, creating significant unmet medical need.

3.3 Development Strategy

  • Phase 1: Dose escalation study in patients with refractory solid tumors (n=30-45)
  • Phase 2: Efficacy evaluation in specific tumor types (n=60-80)
  • Phase 3: Pivotal studies comparing to standard of care (n=400-600)

3.4 Risk Assessment

Based on comprehensive preclinical safety evaluation, potential risks include:

  • Gastrointestinal toxicity (managed with dose modifications)
  • Hepatotoxicity (monitored with regular liver function tests)
  • Potential drug-drug interactions (assessed through PK studies)

3.5 Benefit-Risk Assessment

The potential therapeutic benefit for patients with limited treatment options outweighs identified risks, which are manageable through careful monitoring and dose adjustments.

4. Investigator's Brochure Summary

4.1 Summary of Chemical and Physical Properties

Chemical Name: 2-(4-aminophenyl)-6-methoxybenzothiazole
Molecular Formula: C14H12N2OS
Molecular Weight: 256.32 g/mol
Solubility: Freely soluble in DMSO, moderately soluble in ethanol
Stability: Stable at room temperature for 24 months

4.2 Pharmacology Summary

Mechanism of Action: Selective inhibition of oncogenic kinase with IC50 of 15 nM in vitro

Pharmacokinetics: Oral bioavailability 65%, half-life 8-12 hours, primarily hepatic metabolism

Efficacy: Demonstrated tumor growth inhibition in multiple xenograft models

4.3 Toxicology Summary

Single-Dose Toxicity: LD50 >2000 mg/kg in rodents

Repeat-Dose Toxicity: NOAEL 100 mg/kg/day in 90-day studies

Genotoxicity: Negative in Ames test and micronucleus assay

Safety Pharmacology: No significant effects on cardiovascular or CNS function

4.4 Clinical Starting Dose Justification

NOAEL: 100 mg/kg/day (equivalent to 480 mg/m² in humans)

Safety Factor: 10-fold reduction

Proposed Starting Dose: 48 mg/m² (approximately 1.5 mg/kg)

5. Clinical Protocol Summary

5.1 Study Design

Title: Phase 1 Dose Escalation Study of RX-7890 in Patients with Advanced Solid Tumors

Design: Open-label, single-arm, dose escalation study using 3+3 design

Population: Adults (≥18 years) with histologically confirmed solid tumors refractory to standard therapy

5.2 Study Objectives

  • Primary: Determine maximum tolerated dose (MTD) and dose-limiting toxicities (DLTs)
  • Secondary: Characterize safety profile, pharmacokinetics, and preliminary efficacy

5.3 Dose Escalation Schema

5.4 Safety Monitoring

DLT Definition: Grade 3+ non-hematologic toxicity or Grade 4+ hematologic toxicity during first cycle

Safety Run-in: First patient at each dose level monitored for 7 days before next patient enrollment

Safety Review Committee: Reviews all safety data before dose escalation decisions

7.3 Toxicology Studies Summary

7.3.1 Single-Dose Toxicity

  • Species: Sprague-Dawley rats and CD-1 mice
  • Route: Oral gavage
  • Findings: LD50 >2000 mg/kg in both species
  • Clinical Signs: Mild sedation at highest doses, fully reversible

7.3.2 Repeat-Dose Toxicity

90-Day Rat Study (GLP):

  • Doses: 0, 25, 75, 250 mg/kg/day
  • NOAEL: 75 mg/kg/day
  • Target Organs: Liver (reversible hepatocyte hypertrophy at 250 mg/kg/day)
  • Safety Margins: 15-fold based on AUC comparison

90-Day Dog Study (GLP):

  • Doses: 0, 10, 30, 100 mg/kg/day
  • NOAEL: 30 mg/kg/day
  • Target Organs: GI tract (reversible mucosal irritation at 100 mg/kg/day)
  • Safety Margins: 25-fold based on AUC comparison

7.3.3 Genotoxicity Studies

  • Ames Test: Negative (±S9 activation)
  • In Vitro Chromosomal Aberration: Negative in human lymphocytes
  • In Vivo Micronucleus: Negative in rat bone marrow

7.3.4 Safety Pharmacology

  • CNS: No effects on behavior or neurological function
  • Cardiovascular: No effects on blood pressure, heart rate, or ECG parameters
  • Respiratory: No effects on respiratory rate or tidal volume
  • hERG: IC50 >100 μM (>1000-fold safety margin)

8. Previous Human Experience

8.1 Clinical Experience Summary

RX-7890 is a first-in-class compound with no previous human exposure. This IND application represents the initial human studies for this investigational drug.

8.2 Related Compound Experience

Other kinase inhibitors in the same therapeutic class have demonstrated manageable safety profiles in clinical studies, with common adverse events including:

  • Gastrointestinal effects (nausea, diarrhea) - generally mild to moderate
  • Fatigue and asthenia - manageable with dose modifications
  • Skin reactions - typically Grade 1-2, reversible
  • Laboratory abnormalities - monitored through regular assessments

8.3 Risk Mitigation Strategies

Based on class experience and preclinical findings, the following monitoring plan is implemented:

  • Laboratory Monitoring: Complete blood count, comprehensive metabolic panel, liver function tests at baseline, weekly for first month, then biweekly
  • Clinical Monitoring: Daily assessment during first week, then weekly visits
  • Dose Modifications: Pre-defined criteria for dose reductions and treatment delays
  • Supportive Care: Anti-emetics and anti-diarrheal medications as needed

9. Additional Information

9.1 Environmental Assessment

This IND submission is categorically excluded from environmental assessment under 21 CFR 25.31(e) as clinical research involving small quantities of investigational drug.

9.2 Pediatric Study Plans

Pediatric studies are deferred pending completion of adult Phase 2 studies and establishment of safety and efficacy profile. A Pediatric Study Plan will be submitted as required under PREA.

9.3 Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP)

All clinical trial material will be manufactured in accordance with current Good Manufacturing Practices (cGMP) under 21 CFR Parts 210 and 211.

9.4 Regulatory Compliance

  • Good Clinical Practice: Studies conducted per ICH GCP guidelines
  • Institutional Review Board: IRB approval obtained before study initiation
  • Informed Consent: Written informed consent from all participants
  • Safety Reporting: Adverse events reported per 21 CFR 312.32

9.5 Data Monitoring Committee

An independent Data Safety Monitoring Board (DSMB) will oversee safety aspects of the clinical trial and provide recommendations for study continuation, modification, or termination.

9.6 Risk Evaluation and Mitigation Strategy (REMS)

No REMS is required for this Phase 1 study. Risk mitigation is achieved through careful patient selection, close monitoring, and predefined safety stopping rules.
